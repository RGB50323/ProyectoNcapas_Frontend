# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Sneaker collectors and streetwear enthusiasts in Spanish-speaking markets. They know their brands, hunt for limited releases, and have been burned by fakes before. They use K LAB on desktop and mobile, often in short focused sessions: checking the drop calendar, verifying a pickup, browsing archive pieces. Authentication is not a feature they're curious about; it's the reason they're here.

## Product Purpose

K LAB by Mister K is a curated authentication marketplace for premium sneakers and streetwear. It is not a mass resale platform. The product's core claim: nothing enters the lab that hasn't passed a four-stage protocol. Success looks like collectors trusting the catalog without second-guessing, and private drop members feeling genuinely selected.

## Brand Personality

Crudo, certero, coleccionable.

Raw in aesthetic: no softening, no friendly rounding, no warm neutrals borrowed from consumer apps. Precise in tone: every badge, label, and CTA says exactly what it means. Collectible in positioning: the experience itself should feel like a limited piece.

## References

- **WE GOT KICKS** (wegotkickspr.com): dark foundation, product-first grid, bold streetwear minimalism, confident and approachable. Use as a directional compass for tone and premium-without-aloof positioning.

## Anti-references

- Hype/resale utility platforms (StockX, GOAT): graph-heavy, data-marketplace feel. Too transactional, no curation voice.
- Luxury fashion e-commerce (Net-a-Porter, Mytheresa): excessive whitespace, serif-led, cold high-fashion aloofness. K LAB is street, not runway.
- Generic SaaS product UI: rounded corners, cream backgrounds, icon + heading + text card grids. Completely wrong register.
- Supreme-clone streetwear: red-on-white chaos, maximalist hype noise. K LAB is precise, not loud.

## Design Principles

1. **Autenticidad ante todo.** If a design choice could belong to any marketplace, it doesn't belong here. Every surface should reinforce the verification and curation identity.

2. **Crudo, no pulido.** Rawness is intentional: sharp corners, high contrast, editorial directness. Polish that softens becomes luxury-aloof or SaaS-generic.

3. **El producto manda.** Product imagery and product information leads. Copy earns its space. No decorative text, no intros that restate what the heading already said.

4. **Exclusividad funcional.** The drop mechanics and private access are not features bolted on — they are the brand. Design should make scarcity feel earned, not manufactured.

5. **Confianza a través de precisión.** The authentication protocol is K LAB's core claim. UI language should project expertise and certainty. Mono labels, verification badges, serial references: these communicate craft, not hype.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Readable contrast ratios across all text/background combinations (minimum 4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text). Full keyboard navigation. Semantic HTML with appropriate ARIA labels on interactive elements. Reduced-motion consideration for ticker and product hover animations.
